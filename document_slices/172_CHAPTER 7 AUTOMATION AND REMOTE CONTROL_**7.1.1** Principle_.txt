--------------------
Automatic control, remote operation, instrumentation and central supervision shall be so designed and arranged that the propulsion plant is capable of being operated for 24 hours under normal sea-going condition without manual attendance in engine room to comply with the requirement for the ship with Classification notation (refer to Chapter 1 General 1.3.1).  
One (1) control room shall be arranged in the engine room, where the control console of main engine and the main switchboard shall be installed.  
The instrument gauge and alarm shall be arranged in good order for safety operation and easy maintenance.  
Automatic combustion control and equipment concerned with the boiler shall be arranged adjacent to the boiler.  
Automatic controllers and control valves shall be provided on principal systems for safety operation and easy maintenance.  
The entire automation system shall consist of several decentralized control systems.  
The decentralized control systems shall be used separately for main engine remote control, power management control, stand-by pump changeover control, and engine room monitoring and alarm system, ballast and bilge system.  
The machinery and equipment not specified in this specification shall be served locally by the engine personnel on site, according to needs.  
In case that the contents in the following parts of this section differ from Maker’s standard for specific equipment, Makers’ standard shall be applied in general.  
**UPS for AMS are to be provided. Duration of the batteries shall be minimum 30 min and tested during sea trials.**  
**Serial and analog signals to AMS are to be isolated in equipment / device / sensor side as maker’s standard. Digital signals are to be voltage free contact signals.**
