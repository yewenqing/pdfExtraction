Personnel lifesaving appliances shall be provided by the Builder in accordance with the Rule/Regulations of Classification Society and Flag Authority.  
***Owner supply the SCBA and EEBD recharging compressor. Ship yard supply socket for the compressor,***  
4.6.4.1 Life Jackets and Immersion Suits  
Life jackets and immersion suits shall be provided for 24persons and stowed in each cabin in accordance with the Regulations.  
Each life jacket shall be colored orange and marked with the name of the vessel and port of registry.  
For other spaces, additional life jackets and immersion suits shall be provided and stowed, according to requirement of Classification Society and Flag Authority.  
4.6.4.2 Lifebuoy  
Lifebuoy shall be provided as per rules, general will be as follow:  
|  |  |  |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Location | No. | Accessory |
| Vesselâ€™s side (port and starboard) and stern rail | as per rule | Buoyant lifeline of about 30 m |
| Pilot boarding area | 2 | Self-igniting light |
| Near lifeboat and rescue boat | 2 | Self-igniting light |
| Near accommodation ladder | 2 | Self-igniting light and buoyant life line of about 30m |
| Navigation bridge deck (port and starboard) | 2 | Self-igniting light and self-activating smoke signal with quick releasing device |  
The self-igniting light shall be kept near the lifebuoy to which it belongs.  
The self-activating smoke signal shall be capable of producing smoke of highly visible color for at least 15 minutes.  
Each of the lifebuoy shall be marked with the name of the Vessel and port of registry in black letters.  
Lifebuoy shall be made of rigid polyurethane with plastic cover and fitted with grab line.  
4.6.4.3 Lifeline Throwing Appliance  
One (1) lifeline throwing appliance consisting of four (4) projectiles with four (4) lifelines shall be provided.  
Each projectile shall be capable of carrying the line at least 230 m in calm weather.  
4.6.4.4 Distress Flares  
Twelve (12) sets of rocket parachute flares shall be provided.
