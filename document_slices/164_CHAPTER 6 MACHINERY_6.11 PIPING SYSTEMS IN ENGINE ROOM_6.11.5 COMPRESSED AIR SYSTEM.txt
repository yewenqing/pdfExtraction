Compressed air to be provided to the various services at the following pressure:  
3 MPa : Main engine starting  
Generator engine starting  
0.7 MPa : Miscellaneous usage including air horn  
control air, including the safety air and maneuvering system  
Deck service  
0.55MPa : Hydrophore tank charging  
0.2MPa : Sea chest  
Main air reservoirs and aux. air reservoir shall be charged by main air compressors and emergency air compressor.  
Deck service air reservoir shall be charged by the service air compressor.  
Compressed air for starting the main engine shall be supplied from the main air reservoirs. Generator engines shall be supplied from the main air reservoirs or the aux. air reservoir.  
The deck service compressed air to be supplied from service air reservoir and main air reservoirs via pressure reducing valve.  
The pressure reducing valve shall be provided with a pressure relief valve and a pressure gauge at its outlet and the stand-by pressure reducing valve shall be provided.  
Maneuvering and control air shall be supplied through one (1) pressure reducing valve and one (1) dehydrator from the main air reservoirs.  
Separate airline shall be provided for air horns with locally open valve.  
Air hose connections shall be provided at suitable locations in the engine room.
